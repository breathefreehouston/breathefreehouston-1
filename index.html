Introduction

After my move to Houston, I developed severe asthma symptoms. According to Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America, Houston’s poor air quality makes it one of the hardest cities to live in with asthma. This poor air quality is caused by the high ozone and particulate matter and exposure to the industrial pollutants mostly from Houston Ship Channel and its large factory industry. Also, the asthma rates of Houston have only worsered because of the numerous mold spore infestations because of the devastation of Harvey. The most pressing trigger, however, is the roadway pollution from Houston’s many highways.
